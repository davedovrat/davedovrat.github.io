How Agile?

Specifically: us, right here right now.

Promoting Agility

  • We promote agile development by teaching its techniques,
  • in hopes you won't abuse these techniques,
  • or be abused too much by the teaching.

What we want to see:

  • Analysis: User Stories to Use Cases to Issues to Tasks (and the other way around).
  • Roles: product owner, scrum master.
  • Ceremonies: Scrum Poker, Standups, Sprint planning, Review, Retrospection.
  • Tools: Kanban, Burn up/down charts, backlog.
  • Practices: TDD, CI/CD, pair programming.

The Takeaway

Individuals and interactions

over processes and tools

  • Use the tools available to enter an Agile state of mind.
  • Remember that your team is made of individuals,
    each with their own background, skill-set and character.
  • Try and discard tools and methods until you find what works best for your team.

Working software

over comprehensive documentation

  • TDD where applicable, and as early as possible.
  • Short HLD to get you started.
  • It's better to have an intuitive UI than a detailed manual.

Customer collaboration

over contract negotiation

  • MVP ASAP.
  • Short, frequent sprints.
  • Sprint review.

Responding to change

over following a plan

  • Plan and Retrospect one sprint at a time.
  • Split issues to tasks and tasks to sub-tasks. Divide and conquer.
  • Avoid inter-sprint Scope Creep
    - break it down to more issues and return them to the backlog.
  • Never ending backlog grooming.
